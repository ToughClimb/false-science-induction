# Nature Communications Target Notes

Date: 2026-06-15

## Official Sources

- Nature Communications article page: https://www.nature.com/ncomms/submit/article
- Nature Communications guide to authors: https://www.nature.com/ncomms/submit/guide-to-authors
- Nature Communications submission guide PDF:
  https://www.nature.com/documents/ncomms-submission-guide.pdf

## Target

Submit as a fresh Article to Nature Communications after Nature editorial rejection.
The tone should read as a first submission to Nature Communications, not as a
fallback or rebuttal. Do not mention the Nature rejection in reviewer-visible
materials.

## Official Formatting Snapshot

- No mandatory initial-submission template was found on the official author
  pages; Nature says initial submissions are flexible with respect to format.
- Article targets from the official guide: title if possible 15 words or fewer,
  abstract approximately 150 words and unreferenced, main text no more than
  5,000 words, up to 10 display items, and up to 70 references as a guideline.
- Section headings should be used; subheadings should appear in `Results` and
  `Methods`.
- Methods should be concise and should contain everything needed for
  interpretation and replication; the official guide says they typically do not
  exceed 3,000 words.
- Figure legends should be under 350 words each and should begin with a short
  title sentence.
- High-resolution figures are not required at initial submission, but images
  must be sufficient for referees to assess the data.
- Supplementary information should be a single combined PDF for small tables,
  supplementary figures and text.
- Data availability, code availability, author contributions and competing
  interests statements are required/expected in the manuscript structure.
- The cover letter is not shared with referees and can be used for confidential
  information such as related work in press or submitted elsewhere.

## Positioning

Nature Communications needs broad but field-grounded significance. The pitch is:

- AI-driven discovery loops convert scientific records into experimental action.
- Record binding errors are a distinct reliability boundary: objects and
  measurements can all be real while their association is wrong.
- Coherent misbinding can redirect closed-loop experimental budget while
  marginal label checks remain unchanged.
- The paper provides mechanism, cross-domain computational evidence, and
  operational stop-loss/triage signals.

## Style Guardrails

- Avoid hype, sweeping claims, and "Nature main" rhetoric.
- Use precise first-submission language: "We submit..." not "we resubmit..."
- Avoid AI-fluent filler such as "paradigm-shifting", "underscores",
  "crucial", "robustly demonstrates", "comprehensive", and "we believe".
- Keep limitations visible: controlled loops, retrospective stress replays, no
  claim of natural corruption in public CAMEO/SAMPLE/BEAR archives.
- Preserve all existing claim boundaries and do not add new numerical claims.

## Constraints

- No new experiments.
- No bibliography edits.
- No source edits outside `paper-nature-communications/`.
- Prior Nature package in `paper-nature-main/` is read-only for this task.
