# Nature Communications Submission Checklist

Date: 2026-06-15

## Official Requirements Verified

- Title: if possible 15 words or fewer, no technical terms/abbreviations if
  avoidable.
- Abstract: approximately 150 words, unreferenced.
- Main text: no more than 5,000 words.
- Display items: up to 10 figures/tables for an Article.
- References: guideline up to 70 references.
- Headings: use section headings; subheadings belong in `Results` and `Methods`.
- Methods: concise, typically under 3,000 words.
- Figure legends: under 350 words each.
- Supplementary Information: combined PDF.
- Submission template: no official initial-submission template found; use the
  current manuscript structure and formatting guide.

## Reviewer-Visible Package

- Main manuscript source: `main.tex`
- Supplementary Information source: `supplementary_information.tex`
- Main manuscript PDF: `main.pdf`
- Supplementary Information PDF: `supplementary_information.pdf`
- Cover letter source/PDF:
  `cover_letter_natcomm_20260615.tex`,
  `cover_letter_natcomm_20260615.pdf`
- Figures: `figures/`
- Tables: `tables/`
- Bibliography: `references.bib`
- Author contributions: add in manuscript before data availability.
- Competing interests: add in manuscript before references.

## Nat Commun Positioning

- Present as a fresh Article submission, not a resubmission note.
- Focus on significance for AI-for-science reliability, data provenance and
  closed-loop discovery practice.
- Avoid Nature-main breadth claims and avoid saying the work proves natural
  corruption in deployed archives.
- Preserve the central mechanism: coherent record misbinding can preserve
  marginal labels while changing learned conditional scientific relations and
  closed-loop allocation.

## Claim Boundaries To Preserve

- No universal vulnerability claim.
- No universal stealth claim.
- No claim of natural real-world closed-loop corruption.
- No claim that public CAMEO, SAMPLE or BEAR archives are corrupt.
- No unbounded amplification claim.
- No complete detector or record-level repair claim.
- No live wet-lab validation claim.

## Human Inputs Required Before Submission

- Fill real author names, affiliations, corresponding author email and ORCID
  identifiers in the submission system.
- Add author contributions, funding and acknowledgements for the identified
  final package.
- Decide and enter the required generative-AI-use disclosure.
- Confirm final public or reviewer-accessible code/data archive URL or DOI.
- Re-run PDF metadata scrub after final identified or anonymous PDF generation,
  depending on the selected review mode.
